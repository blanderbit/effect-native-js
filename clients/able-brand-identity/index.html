01
Introduction

This is the rundown of all the elements that make up the able® brand. It's our promise, purpose, personality, product, the way we talk, what we look like, and what we are striving to accomplish. Read, review, absorb, and enjoy. Then stick to these basic guides when working on the able® Brand.

02 A
Brand Framework

We are able®
able® is a renowned service platform helping professional kitchens, businesses and people to meet, eat and evolve.

02 B
Brand Promise

We create meaningful moments for people, business and planet.

02 C
Brand Vision

We truly believe, that considerate and delicious food is a meaningful way to optimise individual well-being, business performance and planet footprint.

We propose an evolving suite of aspiring services to meet the ever-changing needs of modern business and discerning humans.

The perfect meal won’t save the world-but it might just save the day.

02 D
Brand Drivers

Unleashing the big potential behind the simple but powerful brand name of able.

Scandinavian and Altruistic aesthetics drives the design approach and allows us to focus on the true heroes; the kitchens, the food, the people and our shared ambitions—our compassionate side.

Aspiring content and attitude feeds the imaginations of what a truly meaningful lunch might be.

Accommodating the various and changing needs of the user segments, we have to strike a clear balance of when it’s time to reflect on the bigger picture and when we just need to be utterly lean.

All in all, the above manifests in a globally appealing brand.

02 E
Brand Journey

Our brand journey is a tool to help understand and develop new experiences for our customers. Considering the purpose and context of each step is crucial to a successful brand experience.

02 F
Tone of Voice

Our branded voice
It’s when we talk, our audience can feel our personality. How sincere we are and what we actually strive for in our present and future business relationships. And it is equally so, that our branded voice should always reflect, that we acknowledge and respect our audience - no matter their prepositions. Please keep this in mind at all times.

Language—90/10
We excel in the fine balance of global (english) and local (DK, NL, UK). Just like other globally appealing brands. Some headlines, campaigns and key messages might live perfectly in english across the markets, whereas main copy, actual utilities, direct messaging and our everyday language is always kept in the local language: 90% localised + 10% globalised.

Tonality-opinionated and lean
We live by our promise of (…) creating meaningful lunchtime moments for people, business and planet, and we strive to deliver on our brand vision. But we also need to balance our branded voice - relating to the drivers of Altruism, Compassion, Lean, Aspiration and Global Appeal.

We shouldn’t (and cannot) do it all in every piece of content and in every sentence delivered. But we should always consider to balance our opinionated side with the lean and business driven part of our brand.

02 F
Tone of voice

Everything in its own, perfect time: Some messaging is meant to attract, some touchpoints are designed to build relationship with stakeholders or end users and others should just guide as simply and unobtrusively as possible - from the first encounter to a perfect wednesday lunch about to be served, noticed and enjoyed.

In order to create a much needed balance across touchpoints and contexts, we have defined three different levels of messaging.

1.
Branded messaging
Based on our overarching promise and value proposition, we will verbalise the intent and benefits of our business:

Sometimes to end-users

Sometimes to CFO’s (who happen to be humans too).

2.
Emotion before function
Staying close to our concept of meaningful lunchtime moments for people, business and planet, we will identify and and utilise the perfect occasions and touch points to reveal our emotional side - balancing compassion and lean and making our audience acknowledge and appreciate the smallest of details and slightest of emotions.

3.
Function before emotion
The majority of touchpoints (after sign-up) are here to guide, assist, instruct and help our audience as simple and unobtrusively as possible.

In these matters, we should do nothing else than that.

05
Typography
Explore Dia

Dia is a family that captures the diverse spirit of the early Grots while distilling them into a concise and cohesive family that functions as well for text as it does for display copy.

Dia offers a break from the cold and calculated air of contemporary type without sacrificing its conveniences.

06
Materials

Materials plays a key role in the brand experience as this is where the kitchens and customers mainly interact. We should always strive to use sustainable materials such as oak or pine wood, rice paper, bio-plastic, recyced paper and long-lasting fabrics.
